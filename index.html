    About

    I am Lecturer in International Organizations and International Conflict and Cooperation at Department of Political Science at University College London, and Visiting Fellow at LSE IDEAS at London School of Economics.

    My research focuses on foreign policy analysis, emerging powers, and international conflict and cooperation. My book, Turkey, the EU, and the Middle East: Foreign Policy Cooperation and the Arab Uprisings (Routledge 2020) examines foreign policy decision-making processes in Turkey regarding cooperation with the EU during the Arab uprisings and is based on field research involving interviews with diplomats and policy makers in Turkey and at EU institutions.

    At LSE IDEAS, I work on a research project on Turkish foreign policy titled ‘Turkish Foreign Policy in an Age of Regional and Global Turmoil’. Previously, I was a Visiting Fellow at LSE Centre for International Studies (CIS) and I was teaching at LSE International Relations Department and LSE European Institute. 

    At UCL Political Science Department, I teach courses on international conflict and cooperation, wars and violence, global governance and international institutions. Also, I am the Deputy Departmental Tutor, a research collaborator at UCL Global Governance Institute, a member of Conflict & Change, and a member (elected) of UCL Academic Board. 

    I hold a PhD in International Relations from London School of Economics. I completed my PhD as a recipient of the LSE PhD Studentship and as an LSE Middle East Centre Emirates PhD Scholar. I also hold a PGCertHE and an MSc in Politics and Government in the European Union (Merit) from London School of Economics, and a BSc in Politics (with First Class Honours) from University of Bristol. I am also a graduate of Üsküdar American Academy, Istanbul.
